The Swing Equation
df/dt = (Pmech − Pelec) / (2 × H)
df/dt — rate of frequency change (Hz/s)
Pmech — mechanical power from turbine
Pelec — electrical load demand
H — inertia constant (MWs/MVA)
        Gas turbines: typically 3–6 s
What This Means
Pelec > Pmech → rotor decelerates → frequency drops
Pmech > Pelec → rotor accelerates → frequency rises
Low H = frequency changes faster for the same imbalance
10% load step → 1–2 Hz excursion in first second
Key Callout
Small inertia = fast frequency response required.
The governor must act within 1–2 seconds or UFLS trips will cascade.
This is the central challenge of island mode.
Frequency Meter — Bus Response
KEY CONCEPT
Frequency deviation is governed by the power imbalance between mechanical input and electrical load — divided by twice the inertia constant. Gas turbines have low inertia (H = 3–6 s). A 10% load step causes a 1–2 Hz drop within the first second. The governor must respond immediately.
