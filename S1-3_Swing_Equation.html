The Swing Equation
df dt  =  Pmech − Pelec 2H
df/dt — rate of frequency change (Hz/s)
Pmech — mechanical power from turbine
Pelec — electrical load demand
H — inertia constant (MWs/MVA)
        Gas turbines: typically 3–6 s
What This Means
Pelec > Pmech → rotor decelerates → frequency drops
Pmech > Pelec → rotor accelerates → frequency rises
Low H = frequency changes faster for the same imbalance
10% load step → 1–2 Hz excursion in first second
Frequency Meter — Bus Response
Key Concept
Frequency deviation is governed by the power imbalance between mechanical input and electrical load, divided by twice the inertia constant. Gas turbines have low inertia (H = 3–6 s). A 10% load step causes a 1–2 Hz drop within the first second — the governor must respond immediately or UFLS trips will cascade. This is the central challenge of island mode operation.
